# Go-To-Market Strategy: Enterprise AI Coding Governance

## Executive Summary

How to acquire, activate, and expand enterprise customers for AI coding tools with Governance. The method combines **product-led growth** (PLG, bottom-up developer adoption) with **enterprise sales** (top-down procurement) to create a flywheel effect.

## Market Segmentation

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- ~50M professional developers worldwide (higher end)
- AI coding tools: $4.8B current, projected $26B+ by 2030 (we have an existing + growing market)

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- 9M enterprise developers (~18% of devs wpork at companies with 501-5,000 employees, >60% are backend)
- Focus: North America, Western Europe - as a starting point
- ~$5.4B market opportunity (assume $600/dev)

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
- Year 1 target: 12K seats
- Focus: Tech-forward enterprises, fintech, regulated industries that need governance (stickiest + winning this use case opens others)
- ~$8M ARR target (assume ~$600/seat/year)

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: Tech-Forward Enterprise

- Modern tech stack (cloud-native, microservices)
- Existing AI/ML investments
- Public or very late stage companies with 500+ employees ("enterprise") where a non-trivial portion of the employees are engineers (>15%, for example)
- Care about compliance requirements (SOC 2, HIPAA, etc.)

**Examples**: Stripe, Plaid, Notion, Figma, Datadog, Snowflake

### Secondary ICP: Regulated Enterprise
- Industries: Financial services, healthcare, government
- Companies that need: Compliance certifications, Audit trail capabilities
- Global companies (deal with different regulations in different geos)

**Examples**: JPMorgan, UnitedHealth

## GTM Motions

### Motion 1: Product-Led Growth (PLG)

**Strategy**: Developers discover and adopt AI coding tools individually, then expand within organizations by easily upgrading plans.

```
Individual Dev → Team Adoption → Dept Expansion → Enterprise Deal
    (Free)         (Team $)         (Organic)        (Sales $$$)
```

**Tactics**:

1. **Developer Marketing**
   - Technical blog posts on AI coding workflows (X, LinkedIn, etc)
   - YouTube tutorials and live streams
   - Conference talks 

2. **Community Building**
   - Monthly community meetups (share best practices, etc)
   - User-generated content incentives

3. **Viral Loops**
   - "Powered by AI" in commit messages
   - Referral program (1 month free - build on existing example)

4. **Self-Serve Upgrade Path**
   - Easy, in-product prompts when hitting limits

**Metrics**:
- Developer sign-ups: 10K/month
- Free → Team conversion: 5%
- Team → Enterprise upgrade: 30% (in <12mo) 

### Motion 2: Enterprise Sales (top-down)

**Method**: Direct sales to VP/Director level buyers with established need for governance and budget control (go directly to key decision-maker)

```
Lead Gen → Discovery → Pilot → Deployment → Expansion
  (SDR)      (AE)       (SE)     (CSM)       (AM)
```

**Target Accounts**:
- Tier 1 (Strategic): 50 accounts, $500K+ potential
- Tier 2 (Enterprise): 200 accounts, $100-500K potential
- Tier 3 (Mid-Market): 500 accounts, $10-100K potential

**Sales Plays**:

1. **"Governance Gap" Play**
   - Target: Accounts with AI coding tool usage (IDEs, etc)
   - Message: "You're using AI coding tools without visibility - vulnerability"
   - Offer: Free governance assessment
   - Entry: Governance dashboard trial (limited time)

2. **"Compliance Accelerator" Play**
   - Target: Accounts with SOC 2/HIPAA requirements
   - Message: "AI governance for your next audit"
   - Offer: Compliance report generation
   - Entry: 30-day pilot to see ROI

3. **"Developer Productivity" Play**
   - Target: Accounts with developer-optimization initiatives
   - Message: "Measure AI's impact on your team's engineering velocity"
   - Offer: ROI analysis
   - Entry: Team deployment in one engineering group

**Metrics**:
- Win rate: 25% (Team), 35% (Enterprise)
- Average deal size: $80K (Team), $250K (Enterprise)

## Marketing Strategy

### Positioning

**Lead with**: "AI governance for enterprises: Make the most of AI coding tools while having enterprise-grade governance"

**Positioning Statement**:
- WHO: Engineering leaders who want to make their companies and engineers more efficient by using AI tools at scale and safely 
- WHAT: Enterprise Governance is the solution that combines AI coding tools with enterprise-grade governance and compliance

**Key Differentiators**:
1. Agentic AI
2. Built-in governance
3. Safety-first trust

### Demand Generation

**Inbound Channels**:
- Content development (key publications or dev channels)
- Developer community engagement (events, marketing, "how to" webinars)
- Cross-sell with other products

**Outbound Channels**:
- LinkedIn
- Account-based marketing
- Email campaigns to key accounts
- Direct mail and outreach from leaders' accounts

## Sales Process

### Discovery Questions

**Business Context**:
- What's driving your interest in AI coding tools now?
- How are you measuring developer productivity today?
- What compliance requirements do you need to meet?
- Who is involved in this decision? (Map budget decision-makers)

**Technical Context**:
- What AI coding tools are developers using today?
- What's your SSO/identity provider?

**Value Context**:
- If you could measure one thing about AI tool usage, what would it be?
- What would success look like 6 months from now?
- What's the cost of not having visibility into AI usage?

### Pilot Program

**Structure**:
- Duration: 30 days
- Cost: Free (Enterprise-qualified accounts)

**Success Criteria**:
- 80% of pilot users active 
- Governance dashboard accessed weekly (minimum)
- Executive sponsor looped in on results
- Business case documented (can share with other key customers)

**Pilot-to-Paid Conversion Tactics**:
- Weekly check-in calls with ROI analysis
- Executive presentation of results to show impact + generate buy-in
- Limited-time pricing (expires 2 weeks post-pilot)
